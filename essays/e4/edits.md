## 1. Hedge the VPN claim in ¶2 (Argument 1) — HIGHEST PRIORITY

**Problem:** "even VPN applications are already firewall-blocked" is an *absolute* overstatement. VPNs exist to bypass the firewall, and obfuscated/IP-rotating tools partially survive, so "blocked" (absolute) is puncturable — a reviewer can counter "but VPNs do work in China." The firewall *does* aggressively IP-block VPN servers, so the claim is fine as a matter of *degree*, not absolutes.

**Minimal fix (sufficient):** change "are already firewall-blocked" to a hedged, matter-of-degree version. Any of:

> ...and even VPN applications are **largely** blocked by the firewall...
> ...and even VPN applications are **actively targeted** by the firewall's IP-blocking...
> ...and even VPN applications are **heavily restricted** by the firewall...

This turns "VPNs don't work" (false, puncturable) into "VPNs are mostly beaten down by the firewall" (true, defensible). A two-word hedge removes the vulnerability.
