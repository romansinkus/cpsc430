### General Notes

* ethical theory should be **logical** and have an **ethical point of points**
* Kantianism:
  * Should be able to apply it to a scenario without breaking it itself
  * Two categorical imperatives
    * FIRST
    * You should not treat people as a means to an end but rather just as an end in itself
  * Does not care about bad outcome -> there must be a contradiction in order for a rule to not be able to stand
    * Famous contradiction is lying since if everyone else lies then you can't believe everyone
  * If it's a moral rule, it should be applied universally
* Utilitarianism
  * Consequentialist focuses on the consequences
* Kind of Rights
  * Negative right
    * A right that another can guarantee by leaving you alone
  * Postitive right
    * A right obliging other to do something on your behalf
  * Absolute right
    * Guaranteed without exception
  * Limited right
    * A right that may have restricted based on the circumstances
  * Positive rights tend to be more limited
