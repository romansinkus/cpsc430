### General Notes

* The textbook is a good starting point, but should't be treated as the single source of truth
* There needs to be intellectual property, but you shouldn't be able to monopolize certain ideas
* Intellectual property is protected by...
  * (1)Trade secrets
    * No legal protection
    * Never expires -> highest level of protection you can get
    * Reverse engineering allowed
    * May be compromised when employees leave firm
  * (2)Trademark/service mark
    * Does not expire
    * If name becomes a common noun, the trademark may be lost
  * (3)Patent
    * Ensures exclusive right to invention
    * Can prevent others from invention for a certain period of time
    * Use is regulated
    * Defined by government office
* Patent trolls
  * Difficult to control
* Fair Use/Fair Dealing
  * Different criteria for USA and Canada
* Privacy definition:
  * Information or the exchange of information that is actively restricted to a particular group
